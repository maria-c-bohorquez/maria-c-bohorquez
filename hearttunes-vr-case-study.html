Product Case Study

HeartTunes: VR Rhythm System
for Immersive Adolescent Regulation

🎯 THE CONCEPT & CHALLENGE

Traditional adolescent socioemotional training modules often struggle with low student engagement and a lack of immersive, safe environments for intense emotional expression.

HeartTunes explores how immersive, music-based virtual reality gaming can serve as a powerful tool for introspective self-awareness. Developed as a native Unity prototype for the Oculus Quest, the platform translates musical audio tracks and physical virtual drumming interactions into direct metaphors for real-world emotional states.

🌐 CROSS-FUNCTIONAL CO-DESIGN METHODOLOGY

To ensure the product met strict educational standards and resonated with real-world users, we utilized design thinking methodologies in close partnership with Coschool Colombia in Cali — a leading organization in Latin American socioemotional learning.

I facilitated continuous collaborative feedback loops and interviews with educators in Cali to ensure the core virtual reality product features directly supported their pre-existing curriculum goals, successfully aligning hardware expectations with the physical realities of classroom environments.

🧱 TECHNICAL OWNERSHIP & INTERACTION DESIGN

As a technical product lead, I steered the alignment of the game logic within the Unity engine:

  • Product Discovery: Managed user journey maps, product requirements documents (PRD), and feature prioritizations.
  • QA & Validation: Oversaw technical quality assurance loops to ensure high-tempo virtual drumming inputs registered with absolute fluid tracking accuracy.
  • Audio Engineering Strategy: Coordinated hand-in-hand with an international sound designer and musician to construct specialized rhythm tracks. These tracks were engineered using culturally relevant rhythms and precise silences designed to anchor and guide users through intense emotional sessions.

🏆 PRESTIGE OUTCOMES & RECOGNITION

  • Academic Placement: Successfully delivered as a Major Research Project presented to Toronto Metropolitan University in partial fulfillment of the degree of Master of Digital Media.
  • Global Peer Review: Awarded a formal industry invitation to present our interactive UX paradigms, research methodologies, and results at the prestigious international IEEE GEM 2022 (Games, Entertainment, and Media) conference.
